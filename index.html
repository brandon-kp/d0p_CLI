My name is Brandon Probst. I am an 18 year old web developer. I began web development about five years ago. It started out as a simple desire to make my MySpace page look nicer. However, from there, it snowballed. I became one of the top contributors to various message boards, and also authored many tutorials on Skem9.com, FeedTheDragon.net, FreeCodeSource.com, and CreateBlog.com. I was eventually made a moderator on Skem9.com until the site shut down in December of 2008.

From my simple hobby of customizing MySpace profiles with CSS and HTML, I decided to try my hand at something more advanced. I began learning about javascript, and eventually MySQL and PHP. I made what was, in retrospect, production-sized web apps. However, being a dumb teenager and thinking there was no way I would ever attempt to make a living from this, I kept no record of any of it. That's a big disappointment now that I'm working on a portfolio and trying to get work. I can't expect a potential employer to take my word for things I've done in the past.

Since first registering SevenFaces.net in 2006, I've developed message boards, content management systems, search engines, and code generators from the ground up, as well as using various frameworks. Now, in the year 2011 I've decided I want to use my acquired skills to make a living, I'm trying to build a portfolio in an attempt to showcase some of the things of which I'm capable.
